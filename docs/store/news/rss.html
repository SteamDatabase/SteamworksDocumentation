News And Reviews via RSS Feeds For Developers, Publishers, and Curators

In Brief
Post news and game reviews through your Steam Curator or developer homepage automatically from content in an RSS feed.
Level of integration
Requires a one-time set up in Steam and the creation of a regularly updated RSS feed generated by your own content management system.

Setting up an RSS feed allows you to streamline posting news, updates, or reviews from your existing content management platform. You can choose to have Steam automatically post each item in your feed, or you can use it to quickly import your feed content to manually edit before posting.

Requirements

  • If you are a developer or publisher, you'll need to have a homepage set up on Steam. Instructions can be found here.
  • If you are press, video content creator, reviewer, or other form of influencer, you'll need to set up a Steam Curator. Instructions can be found here.
  • Valve will need to enable the RSS feature for your Curator or Developer homepage.

Getting Set Up

To insert your RSS feed, start by visiting your Steam Curator or Developer Homepage on Steam and select the 'admin' tab. In the left list of options, select the new option labeled "Manage my RSS Feeds". Then you will see a field for you to type or paste in your RSS feed URL and see a preview list of posts generated from that feed.

Hit 'save' to save your RSS feed URL.

Automatic Posting

If you enable the option to automatically post from your rss feed, Steam will check your feed every five minutes and automatically post any new articles that appear.

Manual Posting

You may also choose to leave automatic posting turned off and you can post each desired item individually. From your list of RSS posts, you can simply click "Post This Item" and choose whether to stage it as a draft to edit prior to posting, or to post immediately.

Post Visibility

The content from your feed will automatically appear in these locations:
  • Posts will appear on Steam News Hub to players that follow your Steam Curator or Developer Homepage.
  • Posts will appear to anyone that visits your Steam Curator page or Developer Homepage.
  • Posts will appear to to anyone that navigates to /newshub/group/<clanid> (by clicking 'view all' from the curator's page)
  • Posts tagged with game appIDs will appear within those games' news feed for players that follow your Steam Curator or Developer Homepage.

Testing Your RSS Feed

Enter the URL to your RSS feed. It will automatically generate a preview of posts extracted from the feed below. You may preview individual posts.

Updating Public Posts

Once Steam has posted an item from your feed, you can choose to update that item within the Events & Announcements tools in Steam, or on your content management system that generates the rss feed. Steam will detect the updated content in your rss feed and overwrite the original post made on Steam.

Note that changes made to a post in your rss feed will overwrite the values saved on Steam.

RSS XML Fields

Steam can make use of the following fields. Anything else will be ignored.
  • Title
  • Description
  • Link (used as the URL to redirect users to read moreâ€¦)
  • Author (stored but not used)
  • Date used as the date of the article. The later of pubDate or Date field found in the document will be used per post. See below for date format
  • pubDate used as the date of the article. Later of pubDate or Date field found in the document will be used per post. See below for date format
  • Optional: steam:appid to indicate which app specific hub we should surface the information about. To get the appid for a game, just visit the game's store page in your browser and copy the set of numbers from the url

    (NOTE: if you are posting about DLC, a demo, or a soundtrack, please use the appID for the full game. Using the appID for DLC, demo, or soundtrack will have no effect)

Date Format Supported

Steam will parse dates written in RFC 822 format or dates written in ISO-8601 format with the assumed GMT time zone. Note that the RSS standard indicates that the dates should be written in RFC-822 format.

Date and time that are provided must be in the past. It is assumed that any post found within the RSS or Atom feed is immediately visible and publishable by Steam. We will reject articles with future date or times. Note that the date specified cannot be earlier than Sep 12, 2003.

Example RFC 822 Dates:
Tue, 26 May 2020 23:26:48 +0100
Tue, 26 May 2020 23:26:48 PST

Example of ISO-8601 with GMT Time zone:
2020-07-01T10:27:45Z

Example item format:

<?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8" ?> <rss version="2.0"> <channel xmlns:steam="https://store.steampowered.com/"> <title>Test Features</title> <link>https://store.steampowered.com/newshub</link> <description>local news hub testing</description> <item> <title><![CDATA[Post title]]></title> <link>https://www.yourwebsite.com/article/id/etc</link> <author>Jake</author> <pubDate>Tue, 26 May 2020 23:26:48 +0100</pubDate> <description> <![CDATA[<p>This is the main post contents.</p> It's usually your full on article about a game, or can be a portion of the article with a pretty picture. <img width="1920" height="1080" src="https://www.yourwebsite.com"/> It can have images, video and <a href="https://www.yourwebsite.com">links</a>. ]]> </description> <steam:subtitle>A short subtitle (Max 120 characters) that appears at the top of a post when viewing the full post contents</steam:subtitle> <steam:summary>A short summary (Max 180 characters) that appears when a post is presented in a list </steam:summary> <steam:appids> <steam:appid>220</steam:appid> </steam:appids> <steam:recommendation>yes</steam:recommendation> <steam:received_for_free>yes</steam:received_for_free> <steam:received_compensation>no</steam:received_compensation> <steam:recommendation_blurb>This is the short (Max 200 characters) recommendation or review text for the game</steam:recommendation_blurb> </item> </channel> </rss>

Extended Support limited ATOM format:


Once in a while, RSS and ATOM formats get interchanged. There is limited support for ATOM specific elements. We support all of the Steam specific XML within an ATOM entry. Here is an example of supported ATOM elements without any Steam specific elements:

<?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?> <feed xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2005/Atom" xml:lang="en"> <title>Test Site</title> <icon>https://store.steampowered.com/newshub/logo_32x32.png</icon> <updated>2020-07-01T10:27:45-04:00</updated> <entry> <published>2020-07-01T10:27:45-04:00</published> <updated>2020-07-01T10:27:45-04:00</updated> <title>Test Title for Post</title> <content type="html"> <img alt="" src="https://store.steampowered.com/images/logo.jpg" /> <p>This is the main post contents.</p> It's usually your full on article about a game, or can be a portion of the article with a pretty picture. <img width="1920" height="1080" src="https://www.yourwebsite.com"/> It can have images, video and <a href="https://www.yourwebsite.com">links</a>. </content> <link rel="alternate" type="text/html" href="https://www.yourwebsite.com/specific/article_for_this_rss"/> <author> <name>Gordon Freeman</name> </author> </entry> </feed>

Supported HTML / Formatting

Within the 'description' field, you can use the following html tags for formatting:
<a href="foo">Link</a> <br /> <b>Bold</b> or <strong></strong> <i>Italic</i> or <em></em> <u>Underline</u> <ul><li>List Item</li></ul> <ol><li>Ordered List Item</li></ol> <p>Paragraph</p> <img src="foo"> <h1>Heading 1</h1> <h2>Heading 2</h2> <h3>Heading 3</h3> <hr> <iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ClPgQMi_h8c"></iframe> <table> <th><td>Table header cell</td></th> <tr><td>Table cell</td></tr> </table>
Any other html will likely have unexpected results.

Images

The RSS feed parser will automatically pull in the first <img> that it finds in your post and use that image when displaying your post within Steam. These images will be shown at a maximum of 800px wide and can be whatever aspect ratio you wish.

Acceptable content

Our general expectation is that players will be much more receptive to news about games that are available on Steam as well as general PC gaming news (hardware, services, etc.).
  • For PC games available on Steam, you can include the appID so that players can filter out news about specific games if they wish.
  • For items that relate to PC gaming in general and not about a specific game, you can obviously leave the appID fields blank.

Players will likely be much less excited to find news about non-PC platforms and games that are exclusively not on Steam, so weâ€™d prefer to steer clear of those topics in news posted to Steam.

Posting Curator Reviews from RSS

You can use your RSS feed contents to automatically generate reviews for games via Steam Curator system. Please see Curators & Curator Connect for more details on setting up a Steam Curator.

Simply use the item markup within your post item description to express the game it is reviewing, some metadata and your review text.
<steam:appids> <steam:appid>220</steam:appid> </steam:appids> <steam:recommendation>yes</steam:recommendation> <steam:received_for_free>yes</steam:received_for_free> <steam:received_compensation>no</steam:received_compensation> <steam:recommendation_blurb>This is the really short recommendation text for the game</steam:recommendation_blurb>

The field for recommendation can support the following values
<steam:recommendation>yes|no|info</steam:recommendation>

When you post an item automatically from your RSS feed, or manually by clicking "Post This Item", it will generate a Curator recommendation from the metadata and make that post live as well.

Steam Curator visibility

Within the Steam News hub, there will be a couple of ways that players can discover your Steam Curator and choose to follow you for news. We're still working out the details, but it will allow players to find news sources in their native language. You can also direct your existing readership to follow you on Steam to get news blurbs in their News Hub.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q. Do posts appear in the Friends Activity Feed?
A. Posts from RSS feed will not appear in the friends activity list for followers or group members.
