Steam's Summer Showcase of Summer Showcases

Steam's Summer Showcase of Summer Showcases is Steam's hub for summer showcase events (e.g. PC Gaming Show, Xbox Showcase) happening around the weekend of Summer Game Fest. This hub will include a list of each of the participating showcases, along with a carousel of top wishlisted games from each showcase and a clickable banner leading to each showcase's landing page on Steam. Each individual showcase page is built and managed by the organizers of that event, and can host a livestream and lists of the announced games.

Final dates for 2026 are still to be confirmed but will fall around Summer Game Fest in early June.

Important Dates

  • ASAP - Request to participate by filling out the Submission Form at the bottom of our information and rules page here: https://partner.steamgames.com/doc/store/promo/organizers
  • May 21, 2026 - Deadline to confirm your intended participation. Requests following this date will be considered, but inclusion is not guaranteed.
  • May 25, 2026 - Deadline for participants to submit requested assets.
  • May 28, 2026 - Suggested deadline to test and ensure your livestream is working as expected.
  • June 1, 2026 - Deadline to submit your final event page to ensure we have time to review and approve it for publishing.
  • Early June TBD - The hub landing page will become visible, and Valve will send out a press announcement and Steam blog post. Ensure your landing page has been set live prior to this date.

Eligibility

This Summer Showcase is built around the idea of multiple events that are each showcasing a set of new game announcements or updates, unveiled via livestream. To qualify as part of the Steam Summer Showcase, each participating event must be running a livestream at some point with major new announcements.

Event Organizer Requirements

To participate, each event must adhere to the following:
  • Let us know you want to participate by carefully reading the rules and filling out the submission form linked at the bottom of the page here: https://partner.steamgames.com/doc/store/promo/organizers.
  • The Steam promo team will help enable the landing page editor tools, and will send you an asset request with templates and requirements for the assets we need to be able to host and promote your event fully.
  • Once the landing page editor tools have been enabled, you'll need to configure your landing page on Steam by adding your event info, background image, logo, sections for the announced games, and app IDs for all games you'd like to include.
  • Complete the asset request by the noted deadline: you'll be sending us a layered copy of artwork you use on Steam for your event page, including any non-standard fonts.
  • Submit your landing page itself for review by the noted deadline so that our promo team can make sure everything is configured correctly.

Creating a Landing Page on Steam

Each participating event will have a landing page where you'll be livestreaming and showcasing the games that are being announced. To get started creating this page, please visit https://partner.steamgames.com/doc/store/promo/organizers, read the rules, and click the "Submission Form" link at the bottom to get started building your page. When you fill out the form, make sure to include the fact that your event will be part of "Summer Showcases".

Your landing page should have clear branding and description of who is organizing the event, what the event is, and specific dates and times that players would be most interested in (such as any unveiling livestream, or new game playthroughs, etc.). Additionally, you should add app IDs for all games that will be featured on your event landing page.

Providing Layered Artwork and Font Files

We may translate promo materials into the standard set of 29 supported languages on Steam, so we need your layered files to ensure we can include your event in this process. Please plan to send:
  • A layered Photoshop (PSD) file that includes your event logo (smart object preferred) separated from the art. Any text should be editable in a separate “text” folder within the PSD document, so that it may be easily localized by our team.
  • Layered files for any of the banners and images you are including on your sale page and livestream event. This includes Cover Image, Sale Page Background Image, Store Page Banners, and any images you include on your sale page that contain text.
  • Any non-standard fonts used.

Setting Up Your Livestream

Please reference the Livestream section in Steamworks Documentation for additional guidance on how to set up your livestream.

Updating Your Event Landing Page

Once your livestream has concluded, please replace the broadcast embed with a YouTube link to the recorded livestream.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q. What if I'm not ready to set my landing page live by the announcement date?
A. Your event will not appear until you have set your landing page visible. You can set your landing page to be visible, even if the start date of the event is in the future.
