Wider Sale Pages Coming October 6th

We've got an important change coming to sale pages following the Steam Autumn Sale.

Plus, we've recently added support for external UTM links to pass through sale pages to the individual games, so there is a quick description of that feature below as well.

New max page width: 1200px


Starting October 6th, 10am Pacific Time

On October 6th, at the conclusion of the Autumn Sale, we'll be adjusting the general width of sale pages on Steam, allowing the content to scale up to a maximum of 1,200 px wide now. This may impact your sale page if your background art relies on the center column of content being exactly the previous 940px wide.

Sale page UTM support


If you have external links pointing to your sale page, you can now mark up those links with UTM parameters and Steam can now track those through to conversion such as wishlisting and purchase.

UTM reporting is only visible to the developers or publishers of each game. This added support does not provide any additional reporting for event organizers - it merely passes UTM parameters through from external sources to the individual games.

Other third-party events such as showcases, themed sales, or regional events can also add UTM parameters to any external URLs you are using to promote your event. In that case, the developers of participating games will be able to look at their own UTM reports to see conversions on their games that originated with your external links.

If you use different UTM parameters for different posts you make, you can more easily see the impact of each of your posts and how they convert to actions such as wishlisting or purchasing.

For more detail on UTM, please see UTM Analytics Documentation.
